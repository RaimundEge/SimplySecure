Data Encryption Standard

The Data Encryption Standard (DES) is a symmetric-key algorithm for the encryption of electronic data. Although insecure, it was highly influential in the advancement of modern cryptography. DES was published as an official Federal Information Processing Standard (FIPS) for the United States in 1977. DES is insecure. This is mainly due to the 56-bit key size being too small. DES has been withdrawn as a standard by the National Institute of Standards and Technology. The algorithm is believed to be practically secure in the form of Triple DES which uses a 168-bit key. DES uses a fixed block size of 64 bits.
